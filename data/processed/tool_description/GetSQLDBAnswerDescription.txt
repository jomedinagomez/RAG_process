The provided tables contain information about customers, merchants, products, sales, sales details, and stock levels. Here's a brief description of each table:

1. **Customers Table**:
   - **Fields**: `cust_id`, `cust_name`, `cust_email`, `cust_phone`, `cust_address`
   - **Description**: Contains details about customers, including their ID, name, email, phone number, and address.

2. **Merchants Table**:
   - **Fields**: `merchant_id`, `merchant_name`, `merchant_region`, `merchant_address`
   - **Description**: Stores information about merchants, such as their ID, name, region, and address.

3. **Products Table**:
   - **Fields**: `prod_id`, `prod_name`, `price`, `category`
   - **Description**: Lists products available for sale, including product ID, name, price, and category.

4. **Sales Table**:
   - **Fields**: `sale_id`, `cust_id`, `merchant_id`, `date`, `total_price`
   - **Description**: Records sales transactions, detailing the sale ID, customer ID, merchant ID, date of sale, and total price.

5. **Sales Detail Table**:
   - **Fields**: `sales_id`, `prod_id`, `quantity`
   - **Description**: Provides detailed information about each sale, including the sales ID, product ID, and quantity sold.

6. **Stock Table**:
   - **Fields**: `prod_id`, `merchant_id`, `stock`
   - **Description**: Tracks the stock levels of products at different merchant locations, including product ID, merchant ID, and stock quantity.

These tables can be used to manage and analyze customer interactions, merchant operations, product availability, and sales performance.
